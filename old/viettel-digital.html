OVERVIEW

Viettel Money is one of Vietnam's most influential fintech super apps. From payments and banking to transportation and everyday services, its digital ecosystem serves nearly 20 million users, connects 1,000+ partners, and offers 300+ services nationwide.

PROJECT OBJECTIVES

Increasing money flow within Viettel Money by introducing a car-rental service that encouraged users to complete booking and transaction activities directly within the platform rather than relying on external payment providers.

USER PAIN POINTS

Utilized HMWs to open up the user pain points for capable approach for ideating solutions.

Momo struggled to develop smooth and intuitive mobile applications, which lead to steep learning curves that affect customer retention and conversion.

Pain Point How Might We
Users struggle to get the assistance they need. How might we enhance customer services so that users can be assisted in any situations?
Users feel overwhelmed by the amount of information on the transaction page. How might we simplify and visually prioritize critical information on the transaction page to help users avoid missing any terms?
Users find it hard to select their preferred choices because of the app's unorganized filtering sections. How might we redesign the filtering interface to be more organized so users can quickly find vehicles that match their needs?

KEY SOLUTIONS

From Real Pain Points to Actionable Solutions

We ideated solutions through two design sprints and finalized 3 key-selling solutions for real-world users across all generations.

Solution 1

“Quick Support Center” Feature

To enhance customer assistance, I designed a new Quick Support Center feature. This feature provides users with three primary support options:

  • Hotline support available daily from 7:00 AM to 10:00 PM
  • Messaging support for more flexible communication
  • Hotline access to car insurance support

A comprehensive FAQ section also helps users quickly resolve common questions without contacting support directly.

Solution 2

Streamlined Transaction Page

To improve the transaction page experience, I structured information into clearly defined categories:

  • Car rental policies
  • Rental requirements
  • Important notices
  • Change and cancellation policies
  • Potential additional fees

Each category is presented in a dedicated section so users can locate relevant information quickly and understand key details with less effort.

Solution 3

Dual-layer Filtering System

The detailed filtering system includes a wide range of options such as car motion type, energy type, passenger capacity, brand, price range, and car type.

This diverse selection empowers users to choose a vehicle that aligns with their preferences and specific requirements in different circumstances.

04. USER TESTING + ITERATION

Cognitive walkthrough and design improvements

I conducted cognitive walkthrough with 10 participants, assigning them two main tasks. Through this user testing, I identified design elements that caused friction in the user experience. Using these insights, I refined key app features to enhance usability and improve overall user satisfaction.

— Task 1

Rent an electric car priced above 1 million VND

90% of users achieved the right result. 70% associated the correct action with the effect they were trying to achieve, while 30% missed the filtering options.

— Task 2

Change renting date in the middle of the process

60% of users achieved the right result. Several participants expected to adjust the date directly from Step 3 instead of returning to earlier steps.

ITERATION

Informed 2 design decisions based on customers' feedback

Synthesizing user feedbacks and their experiences within the app, I iterated my initial designs to help users go directly on their golden path. Here are my design improvements:

Add Filter Options on the top and Rename "Sort By" to "Filter" for better clarity

Allow users to adjust rental date and location in Step 3 without having to go back to Step 1 or Step 2

05. FINAL DESIGN

Being mindful with UI design

Building on the existing design system of the Viettel Money app, I focused on creating a visually intuitive and user-friendly interface. My goal was to ensure accessibility for all users, including older adults, aligning with stakeholder requirements.

Key takeaways

Since this was my first internship project, I found myself truly enjoying the process of ideating, designing, and conducting user usability testing. Here are my 3 major takeaways:

1.

Communicate well with different teams

Learn how to communicate my design decisions clearly with Product Managers, Engineers, and other stakeholders.

2.

Decisions need to be based on reality

I found out that instead of designing an app intuitively, my designs need to be based on what users truly wants and needs through user interview and usability testing.

3.

Shortcuts accelerate user decision-making

Providing shortcuts to key actions without having to return to previous steps ensures a more flexible and streamlined experience.
