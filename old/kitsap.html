OVERVIEW

Expanding awareness of a federal social benefits program. This project aimed at increasing awareness and enrollment in the Lifeline program among underserved Kitsap County residents lacking reliable mobile device access.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Many Kitsap County residents who lack reliable access to mobile devices or internet services face limited awareness and utilization of programs like Lifeline, restricting their ability to access real-time transit information.

01. DISCOVERY

Building trust to reach underserved communities

We conducted a literature review of 25+ articles on the digital divide, mobile phone access, and Lifeline program participation. Initial outreach challenges led us to identify secondary stakeholders — nonprofits — who helped us access participants. We successfully conducted 8 in-person interviews at the Salvation Army Center.

STAKEHOLDER INSIGHT

"

People I assist with say that the Lifeline phones don't come with enough data.

— Anton Preisinger, Director of Northwest Hospitality

RESEARCH PROCESS

Primary vs secondary interviews & in-person vs online distribution

We used InsightSynth AI to identify key patterns across interview data and visualized interview distribution to understand reach and coverage.

KEY INSIGHTS

4 key research insights identified after synthesizing interview data across all participants.

02. DEFINE

Four key barriers preventing Lifeline adoption

Through synthesis of our interview data we identified four primary barriers that prevent eligible residents from enrolling in the Lifeline program.

Digital Literacy

Text-heavy qualification content creates barriers for older adults and non-English speakers.

Lack of Awareness

Limited understanding of program benefits despite name recognition among some residents.

Trust

Skepticism about program legitimacy and concerns regarding data privacy discourage sign-ups.

Eligibility

Confusion about qualification requirements; unawareness that SNAP/Medicaid enrollment provides automatic qualification.

03. PERSONAS

Two personas representing residents without mobile access

Based on our research, we created two personas representing middle-aged individuals without mobile phones who need reliable connectivity for transportation or job applications.

USER PERSONAS

Grounded in real interview data, these personas guided our design decisions and helped communicate user needs to stakeholders.

04. ETHICS

Designing with dignity and community trust

Working with vulnerable populations required us to center ethical considerations throughout our research process — from recruitment to data handling.

Community Partnerships

Prioritizing in-person outreach through established nonprofits to build trust before conducting interviews.

Clear Communication

Using plain, jargon-free language and visual aids to ensure informed consent from all participants.

Privacy & Dignity

Respecting participant privacy, anonymizing all data, and centering dignity throughout the research process.

Accessible Design

Designing solutions that are accessible to non-technical audiences across age groups and language backgrounds.

05. HOW MIGHT WE

Framing the design challenge

With our research synthesized, we framed a single How Might We question to guide the 9-week design phase and usability testing planned for Spring quarter.

HOW MIGHT WE

"

Design an accessible solution for non-technical audiences that helps them better understand, trust, and enroll in the Lifeline program?
