THE PROBLEM (FROM THE POV AS A COLLEGE STUDENT)

Learners are overwhelmed with text-heavy materials that are hard to process on the go or during multitasking.

PREVIEW

PROJECT OBJECTIVES

Provide users a seamless experience in managing and utilizing materials efficiently and systematically — helping them save time, boost productivity, and make knowledge accessible to everyone through audio-based podcasts.

01. DISCOVERY

Understand who is struggling and why

I started this project from my own struggle as a college student — drowning in dense readings I couldn't keep up with on the go. Through research, I realized there were many more students out there facing the same challenge.

SURVEY

Surveyed 50 students to validate the problem

I sent out a Google Form to 50 college, master's, and PhD students to gather data on their study difficulties and the platform features they wished existed.

USER PERSONAS

Two behavioral personas representing busy learners

From the survey insights, I built two behavioral personas — busy individuals with heavy reading loads, struggling with text-heavy materials and seeking to process dense texts efficiently while multitasking.

02. DEFINE

Frame the core problem and explore solutions

The core issue surfaced clearly: the lack of customization to user needs, such as personalized audio summaries. From there, I brainstormed twelve actionable directions before narrowing them down.

IDEATION

Considered 12 actionable ideas before landing on audio

Among all directions explored — including converting articles into comic-based formats and game-based learning with puzzle games — the audio file conversion platform emerged as the strongest fit for users' multitasking lifestyle.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

NotebookLM, Google's AI tool, has clear gaps

NotebookLM offers limited flexibility in adapting to diverse learning styles and lacks seamless integration for creating user-specific content formats — leaving room for a more personalized audio experience.

UNIQUE SELLING POINTS

Granular control and personalization, end to end

Users have full control over their audio experience — from fine-tuning preferences before generating a podcast to adjusting tone, length, and number of hosts. They can edit the length of audio summaries, adjust the tone between formal and conversational, and choose the number of hosts.

03. PROTOTYPE

Design an intuitive content-to-audio experience

The vision: a platform where every user can engage with content in a way that feels intuitive and efficient — AI-powered audio summaries and flashcards built around accessibility for everyone.

USER FLOW

A simplified 4-step user flow

I distilled the experience into four essential steps: upload sources, prompt the AI with additional context, fine-tune preferences, and generate.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Three main sections, designed for flow

The main navigation lives across "Create a podcast," "Recent Pods," and "Spaces." Users can upload up to 50 different types of sources simultaneously, and shortcuts between existing podcasts and creating new ones keep navigation seamless.

CORE SCREENS

Four core screens shape the product

Home Dashboard — an organized overview with pinned and recent items and customizable categories.

Create a New Pod — input any type of source and receive real-time recommendations on additional helpful sources.

Customization — granular customization inputs before users confirm preferences and generate.

Latency Page — shows AI generation progress alongside contextualized flashcards users can interact with while waiting.

04. USER TESTING

Cognitive walkthrough and design improvements

I ran in-person and remote testing sessions over Zoom with Washington students. Two key feedback themes emerged that shaped the next iteration.

— Feedback 1

Streak system felt stressful, not motivating

"I feel like I'd get really stressed out if I had to study every single day just to keep the streak going." Users shared they wouldn't engage with the streak feature, so I removed it entirely.

— Feedback 2

Customization felt too dense

"The space for the fine-tuning option feels quite small, making it hard for me to click on each one." I refined the back button, reduced fine-tuning to the 2 most used features, and added breathing room.

ITERATION

Refined design decisions based on feedback

Synthesizing user feedback and observed behaviors, I iterated on the initial designs to help users move through the flow with less friction. Here are the key design improvements:

  • Initial logo changed to match the overall theme
  • Limited the homepage navigation to 3 clear sections
  • "Recent Pods" refined to contain only generated podcasts
  • Added a 3-step progress bar for process tracking
  • Showed captions side-by-side with generated podcasts

05. FINAL DESIGN

Pods, designed to feel truly your own

The final platform empowers users to create podcasts that feel truly their own — from AI-generated flashcards while content loads, to deep audio customization controls.

TIMELINE

Key takeaways

Pods sharpened my product instincts in three key ways:

1.

Not always more is better

I initially offered many customization features to help users tailor podcasts. Too many options overwhelmed users, making choices harder and the design less user-friendly.

2.

Adapt to necessary changes

Across two design versions and valuable user testing insights, adapting to necessary changes made the final designs more inclusive and accessible — better matching users' needs.

3.

Align goals throughout the process

Keeping the goal of boosting user customization in mind helped maintain focus and develop features that genuinely cater to user needs.
